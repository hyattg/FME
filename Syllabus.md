## Course Description

This intensive field course provides hands-on experience investigating microbial ecology in natural ecosystems. Students will design and conduct field experiments examining key microbial metabolisms including phototrophy, nitrogen fixation, iron oxidation, sulfate reduction, and methanogenesis. Through hypothesis-driven research at Cranberry Lake Biological Station, students will develop skills in field sampling, microscopy, experimental design, data analysis using R and AI tools, and scientific communication. The course emphasizes rigorous methods, appropriate controls, and synthesis of field observations with concepts from EFB 303: Introduction to Environmental Microbiology.

---

## Learning Outcomes

- Design and execute field experiments with appropriate controls, replication, and rigorous sampling methods
- Develop testable hypotheses about microbial ecology in natural systems
- Collect, analyze, and interpret ecological and microbiological data from field environments
- Communicate scientific findings through written reports and oral presentations
- Recognize and characterize key microbial metabolisms (nitrogen fixation, iron oxidation, phototrophy, methanogenesis, sulfate reduction) in their natural habitats
- Understand how environmental factors (oxygen, pH, temperature, light wavelength, nutrient availability) structure microbial communities
- Identify relationships between microbial processes and their geochemical or biological context
- Connect microbial cell and colony morphology to ecological function
- Properly collect environmental samples while minimizing ecosystem disturbance
- Measure relevant environmental parameters in field settings
- Perform microscopy and staining techniques on environmental samples
- Culture environmental microbes using selective and differential media
- Use enrichment techniques to isolate specific physiological groups
- Assess water quality using microbial indicators
- Evaluate antimicrobial resistance in environmental bacteria
- Recognize the ecological and economic importance of microbial processes

## Lab Safety

This course involves laboratory work with microbiological samples, chemical reagents, and laboratory equipment. Students must follow all biosafety protocols, wear appropriate personal protective equipment (lab coats, gloves, eye protection), practice proper aseptic technique, and handle all biological materials safely. Students must immediately report any spills, exposures, or safety concerns to the instructor or TA. A comprehensive lab safety briefing will be provided on the first day of class before any laboratory work begins.

---

## Field Safety

This course involves fieldwork in natural environments that may present hazards including uneven terrain, water hazards, weather exposure, and encounters with wildlife. Students must follow all safety protocols, use appropriate personal protective equipment, and immediately report any injuries or safety concerns to the instructor. A detailed field safety briefing will be provided on the first day of class.
