# Field Microbial Ecology

**A 10-day field course at Cranberry Lake Biological Station**

SUNY College of Environmental Science & Forestry | July 5--17, 2026 | 3 Credits

## Overview

This intensive field course places undergraduate biology majors in the forests, wetlands, streams, and soils of the Adirondack Park to investigate microbial metabolisms through hands-on, hypothesis-driven research. Students process identical environmental samples through both direct observation and classical pure-culture techniques, learning why different methods tell different stories about the same microbial community.

No prerequisites. Open to all biology majors.

## What Students Will Do

- Survey iron-oxidizing bacteria at natural seeps and characterize their geochemistry
- Investigate nitrogen fixation in Speckled Alder and Sweetgale root nodule symbioses
- Assess microbial water quality of Cranberry Lake using standard methods
- Capture and test methane from anoxic wetland sediments
- Enumerate antibiotic-resistant bacteria from environmental samples
- Analyze data in R and present findings in written and oral formats
